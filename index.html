About Me

I am a Research Scholar (PhD) in the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at IIT Bombay, where my doctoral work sits at the intersection of quantitative finance, market microstructure, and asset pricing.

My research investigates how financial markets process information, how prices are formed, and how asset returns can be explained through rigorous empirical and theoretical frameworks. Beyond my core PhD thesis, I am increasingly drawn to climate finance — examining how climate risk is priced in financial markets and the role of green finance in facilitating the transition to a sustainable economy.

I enjoy bridging the gap between rigorous academic theory and real-world financial phenomena, using a combination of econometric techniques and computational tools to extract insights from large financial datasets.

Research

My work spans quantitative finance with a particular focus on how markets function, how prices reflect information, and how financial systems interact with climate change.

Market Microstructure

Investigating the mechanics of financial markets: how trades are executed, how liquidity is provided, and how information is incorporated into prices. I study the role of market makers, order flow, and the determinants of bid-ask spreads in both equity and derivatives markets.

Order Flow Liquidity Price Discovery High-Frequency Data

Asset Pricing

Examining the cross-section of expected returns and the empirical validity of asset pricing models. My work explores risk factors, anomalies, and the quantitative underpinnings of return predictability across different asset classes.

Risk Factors Return Predictability Empirical Finance Factor Models

Climate Finance

Exploring the growing field at the intersection of finance and climate science. I investigate how climate-related risks and opportunities are priced in financial markets, the role of ESG factors in portfolio construction, and how capital markets can support the green transition.

Climate Risk ESG Green Finance Sustainability
